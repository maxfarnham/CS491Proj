    About Motherboard

    Written by Motherboard staff

    December 4, 2009 // 09:42 AM EST

    The future is wonderful, the future is terrifying. We should know, we live there. Whether it's on the ground or on the web, Motherboard is traveling the world to uncover the stories that will define what's coming next. New technologies, cultures, and discoveries are constantly reshaping this old planet of ours. And it's happening faster than ever before.

    With in-depth blogging, longform reporting, and video journalism, Motherboard investigates the news and events that are already affecting the years to come. We want to help you get your hands on tomorrow. Beyond that, we strive to bring our audience an honest portrait of the futures we're racing towards.

    Write for Motherboard

    Thanks for your interest in writing for Motherboard! We have contributors all around the world. We're looking for smart, highly original science and tech stories of any length:  ​the mystery of a 1987 television hack, perhaps, or ​your complicated relationship with Snood—we're also into things like ​cryon​ics, ​seasteading, the science of training pigeons, ​NSA spy gadgets, ​Foxconn's internship program, and of course, the ​forest of dead bodies in Tennessee where scientists study how living tissue decays.

    We also love profiles: ​the man who created Tetris, the programmer who spent ​10 years writing an operating system for God, and the YouTube star who will eat anything, to name a few.

    Please email editor@motherboard.tv with "PITCH" in the subject, and include working headline, a three to four sentence description of the story (note: this should not consist of a series of questions), and an estimated word count.

    Write for Terraform

    ​Terraform, Motherboard's science fiction section, seeks submissions from established and aspiring authors. To learn more, jump over to  ​How to Submit Your Stories to Terraform.
