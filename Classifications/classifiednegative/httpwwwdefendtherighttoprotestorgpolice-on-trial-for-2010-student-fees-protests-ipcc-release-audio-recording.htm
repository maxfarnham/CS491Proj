About Us

About the campaign

Defend the Right to Protest was launched in response to violent police tactics and arrests at the student protests of November and December 2010, with the support of activists, MPs, trade unionists, student groups and others. We campaign against police brutality, kettling and the use of violence against those who have a right to protest. We campaign to defend all those protestors who have been arrested, bailed or charged and are fighting to clear their names.

Police Officers facing charges from 2010 student fees protests – IPCC release audio recording

May 14, 2015 ~ Leave a Comment ~ Written by admin

9uth december 2010 students protest as parliament votes on fees

The trial of three police officers Andrew Ott, Calvin Lindsay and Thomas Barnes charged with offences relating to the 9th December 2010 student protests has been ongoing since last Tuesday at Southwark Crown Court.

PC Ott is accused of hitting student protester William Horner with his shield, and all three are charged with conspiracy to pervert the course of justice.

They deny the charges. (for more detail see  Simon Israel’s report for Channel 4 news).

At the heart of the case is an audio recording of the incident in question – taken from the recording device which PC Ott was wearing throughout the day.

Today the IPCC made that audio recording available.
