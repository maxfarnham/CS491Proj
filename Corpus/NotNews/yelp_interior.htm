        Sorry to be the first downer, but this place is not that good.  The quality of the corned beef is just not there.  It's a little thin and a little tough for my taste.  I'm not even sure they're curing it in-house.  It seems like mass produced deli meat (think Boar's Head, etc) instead of what I look for in corned beef, which is thicker slices and tender crumbling meat.  Some of that connective tissue needs to be softened and the fat should be partially rendered and buttery.

        I just found the Mother of Holy Grail when it comes to a corned beef sandwich. The world's best corned beef sandwich is not in NYC it is at The Corned Beef Factory in Chicago!  Yes, I have had my share of many, many NYC corned beef sandwiches, so I know what I am talking about here. There are two kinds of people in this world - those who settle for mediocre corned beef and those who settle for award winning corned beef. This is definitely the crème de la crème corned beef. The meat was piled high, layer after thin layer, on fresh soft bread. The sandwich was simple, flavorful and tender. It was perfectly dimidiated and not overly salsuginous. The service was effusive and hospitable.

        I rarely give a 5-star rating on Yelp. This joint reminded me of the Corned Beef sandwich that I had at Ben's Best Kosher Delicatessen in Queens, NY, which is considered one of the top five deli joints in NYC for corned beef sandwiches. What I appreciated here is their limited menu, because this means the product provided is high quality. Oftentimes, when I see a place with tons of items on their menu, I know the quality isn't going to be great. The price-point, sandwich and service are all worthy of 5-stars. I had to take the other half of my sandwich home, because I couldn't even finish the entire thing. It is massive! My husband enjoyed his sandwich. He scarfed it down in record time, and even had room for the honey puffs. I thought he was going to ask me for a Tums later, but he never did!

        There was a discussion on Yelp Talk (a thread) where people were raving about The Corned Beef Factory. Of course, I had never heard of this place before. Upon the recommendation and high praises from the Yelp community, we decided to hit it up on Friday for lunch. Wow, it was definitely well worth the drive.

        P.S.
        Hey, this isn't NYC styled corned beef. If you are accustomed to thick and crumbly slabs of corned beef (like a steak sandwich) don't bother coming here. You'll just end up writing a "Debbie Downer" review! BTW, I did NOT receive any free-bies here. I did pay for my own lunch, and I'm still giving this joint 5-stars.

        Finally got a chance to try this place and it did not disappoint. Their "no-frills" approach makes getting a high quality sandwich quick and easy, but it's standing counter only, so if you're looking to sit down you're better off trying some place else.

        I split two sandwiches with a buddy, so I tried the Reuben (which we got with mozzarella instead of swiss) and Corned Beef (with mustard and pickle), which come with homemade chips and a pickle. All that might sound/look intimidating, but both the corned beef and bread are surprisingly light so I ended up killing it all. Quickly.

        The Reuben was amazing. The perfect balance of the dressing, sauerkraut, and meat. The Corned Beef was tasty as well but I doubt I will get that again - compared to the Reuben it was just a little plain. Plus the Reuben was so damn good. The homemade chips were great - sliced thin, not greasy, not too salty. And the pickle was great too - just a crisp, classic deli pickle.

        The one thing I didn't like is that you don't really get a sense of what your options are with the sandwiches. The staff is very helpful/friendly and more than willing to tell you what you can add/substitute with the sandwiches, as we learned, but I think it would be nice to be able to "see it." I realize this might go against their simple/clean approach but I think it would help in the long run.

        Overall, I can't wait to go back and try either the italian beef, pastrami or hot dog.

        Also, I'm a little wary when it comes to rye bread, but their's doesn't have too strong of a rye flavor, so if you're in the same boat when it comes to rye, don't let that influence your decision.
