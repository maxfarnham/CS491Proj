                                                              I was reading a different article about these new signs - in that article they surveyed people about the new signs. Overwhelmingly, people found them harder to use.

                                                              When I looked at the "tree" of signs, I quickly understood why it is easier to navigate. Just as an article has a headline and subheads to find sections (or a book has a table of contents), a group of signs has a very different structure -- allowing one type of information to be quickly "looked up".                                                               The new sign forces a different hierarchy of information - and not the information that is most important to me as I'm driving along a street.
