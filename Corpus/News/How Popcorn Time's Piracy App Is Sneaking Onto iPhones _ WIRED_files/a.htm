                                                                Since no one is stealing movies, it seems like a silly question. Copyright infringement is not theft. It's an IP crime that falls in a completely different category.
                                                                Ideally, content owners (usually a separate entity from content creators) would enable people to purchase rights to access the content in an easy manner at reasonable prices.                                                                 The situation is improving, though.                                                                     nerdrrage mathanacey • 3 days ago

                                                                    The idea that piracy comes only from a broken distribution system is breaking down as Netflix and Amazon expand. Now HBO is jumping in with their own legal streaming service. One day, this is how everyone everywhere with a decent internet connection will access entertainment: in a movie theater or via streaming. Those are the two industries that are economically healthy. Broadcast, cable and DVD are faltering and will be driven to extinction by streaming. And there will still be piracy because some people will always want something for nothing.

                                                                    Oh, and there will still be exclusive deals. Let's say that the global streaming services are Netflix, Amazon and Google (YouTube brand or otherwise). They all want a competitive advantage over the others, so they will try to lock in exclusives. HBO can probably survive as a smaller, premium-brand player alongside the behemoths. Their content will continue to be exclusive to them, because that's their one and only competitive advantage.

                                                                    The exclusives will probably go out of date after a while, and then the content will be distributed to anyone who will pay, like HBO has licensed their back catalog to Amazon. The impatient will have to subscribe to all three services, the patient will be rewarded by lower cost, but honestly this system will product more great content than any sane person can consume anyway.

                                                                    Popcorn Time may still exist, but it will not have any impact on the development of this new streaming business model for a simple reason: no feedback loop. A business model needs that loop - that's the "model" part - whereby money loops around from the customer to the distributor to the content maker, who uses it to make more content, closing the loop. Piracy is not part of the loop, which will need to develop through legit means. Piracy is basically parasitism, but benign parasitism, because it's not stopping the healthy development of a legit streaming business model.
