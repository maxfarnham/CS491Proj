                                                                                                                                danielravennest AJ • 9 hours ago

                                                                                                                                But this time it's changing because humans are excavating old fossil fuels and burning them, and cutting down forests and burning them too. The reason we know it's people doing it is the shift in Carbon-13 ratios. Plants slightly prefer the lighter Carbon-12 isotope, because it diffuses and reacts faster. Along with the absolute increase in CO2 in the atmosphere, the ratio of C-13 is also changing, indicating the source is not volcanoes or other inorganic sources. The only organic sources large enough to account for the total increase are fossil fuels & deforestation, both human-caused.

                                                                                                                                Why should you care? If you live less than 75 meters ( 250 feet ) above current sea level, you could end up underwater. If you don't live in low lying areas, the people who do may decide to move in. Climate zones can shift, affecting the food and water supply. When previous climate changes happened, like the last Ice Age, there were very few people around to care, and they were hunter-gatherers, so moving was easy. Moving a big coastal city like New York, London, or Tokyo (all at sea level) isn't so easy.
