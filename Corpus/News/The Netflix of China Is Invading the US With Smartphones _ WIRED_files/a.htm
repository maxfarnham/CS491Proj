                                              It's not even so much about angering customers as just being ignored in a noisy and crowded market. I would hate to be a Netflix competitor now trying to break into streaming, an industry where there is bound to be just a handful of winners that operate globally. Each new territory that Netflix takes over means millions of new subscribers to funnel cash to Netflix to help win the bidding wars for the glitzy Hollywood content that is the main attraction of streaming. They're on a snowball path now, hard to see how anyone can stop them.

                                              The only companies that have a hope to compete are already big and safe (Amazon, where streaming is just a loss leader to get new customers to sell stuff to) and/or have glossy brands (HBO). I wouldn't even count on Hulu to go the distance, despite having big Hollywood backing. And speaking of that, HBO's entry into streaming signals that Hollywood has woken up and realizes that streaming is the future, so they're all going to jump in with their own services. It's going to become a lot more competitive and crowded very soon.
