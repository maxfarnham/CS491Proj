                                                          Christopher R Weiss • a day ago

                                                          A biological weapon is absurd. Most viruses affect multiple species. Another foreign species would also have unintended consequences. I wonder how many native animals are killed by poison baits? The problems with feral species are related to people. Even if all feral cats were to disappear overnight, thousands of new ones would appear within a few months and millions within a few years. Cats escape and become feral, people abandon cats, etc. You cannot stop the introduction of feral cats without banning all cats on the continent.

                                                          Population controls through spaying and neutering are the best options. Cats that can't reproduce won't make the problem worse. License cats and charge large fees to breed cats, and you can start to slow the growth of the feral population. Rendering feral but functional cats infertile will also lower reproduction rates by having fertile cats waste breeding opportunities.

                                                          There is no quick solution. Culling, sterilization, and vigilance are the only options.
