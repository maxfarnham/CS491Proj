                                                                          Higher_Ground AaronHuertas 7 days ago

                                                                          that poll asked

                                                                          "Thinking about what is said in the news, in your view is the seriousness of global warming -- generally exaggerated, generally correct, or is it generally underestimated?"

                                                                          That tells us precisely nothing about whether people believe or deny in climate change - and goes directly to the political heart of the matter with regard to 1) media exposure and 2) risk associated with climate change.

                                                                          It's not asking if people believe that it exists or not. It's asking if they think we take it seriously - based on what we see in the news.

                                                                          But back to your point - the way the question is phrased could have a lot to do with how honestly people answer the question. For someone who does not really think it's a problem - watching FOX News all day would indeed reaffirm your beliefs, and you would then assume the person would say "generally correct" as it confirms to their worldviews. However, I think most educated republicans would instead interpret the question to mean "what do you think of the main stream media's coverage of global warming" and therefore it would make more sense for them to say it's exaggerated.

                                                                          I really don't like how poll questions are phrased and then used to extrapolate meanings that weren't explicit in the responses. I can't blame you for posting the link - even the headline doesn't accurately reflect the poll's question with regard to the phrasing "what is said in the news" and "seriousness".
