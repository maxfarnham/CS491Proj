                        FamilyManFirst • 3 hours ago

                        I've read a few accounts of the debacle of the initial Affordable Health Care Act website rollout and the people that fixed it. My takeaway: government IT contracts don't even have the faintest hint of accountability, nor are they awarded on anything even remotely resembling competence or capability. Winning a government IT contract is all in who you know and how well you know the process. One or two (or three) companies have won contract after contract without managing to deliver properly on a single one; they still continue to win contracts.

                        This is anathema to "the tech industry" whose individuals prize a meritocracy. We don't always get it, but that's our ideal.

                        If the White House CTO wants "the tech industry" to "show up," she needs to make wholesale changes to the ways that government IT contracts are awarded and rewarded. Otherwise the only companies that will apply will be the ones that prioritize feeding at the government trough at the (now obvious) expense of technical ability and capability.
