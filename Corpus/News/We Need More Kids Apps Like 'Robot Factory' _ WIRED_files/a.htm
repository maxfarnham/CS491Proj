            I think this app is what inspires young minds to create and learn. Not only does it offer a stimulus for the imagination but it also encourages young curious minds to think beyond the actual bit of putting the robot together, it opens their eyes to the capabilities and 'physics' if you will, of the robot / masterpiece they've created, it helps them to 'think ahead' and improve on their robots ( and systematically their knowledge and thought patterns). Although some parents might disagree because their children seem to be spending more time behind a screen than frolicking around outside, but in their defence, this app is a constructive learning tool, which opens more than one door on many intellectual levels. I wouldn't quite encourage it's implementation in school activities because it might distract them from their actual pre-scribed work that needs to be done, but I don't see why it can't form part of extra-curricular activities. Who knows, this might be the app that helps mankind develop real robots.
