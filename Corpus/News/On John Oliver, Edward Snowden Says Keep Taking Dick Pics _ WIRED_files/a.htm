                        garyisabusyguy JaitcH • 8 days ago

                        Warrant less wiretaps were allowed between 1928 and 1967, with meta-data singled out as not requiring a warrant because it was information freely given to the phone company, who then owned it in a court case in 1979. This was all codified in the Electronic Communications Privacy Act of 1986 where meta data was identified as Pen Registers

                        Since that time the use of Pen Registers has been a primary means of detecting criminal networks and setting the probable cause for further warrants to allow wiretaps

                        It is important to understand the history behind these laws in order to realize that this has been going on for much longer than the Patriot Act. There have been other information networks in operation since WW2 that have had legal access to all communications that cross national borders, Echelon was a closely held secret that was first mentioned in the press in the mid 80's.

                        Anybody who was surprised by snowden's revelations what simply not paying attention
