                                                                                      hambdiscus NegroSven 5 hours ago

                                                                                      There are shaft-drives available but turning two 90-degree angles involves mitre gears and for smoothness, strength and quietness, spiral bevel gears are better.

                                                                                      Unfortunately, such gear sets need to be precisely meshed and that means some means of sub-millimeter adjustment must be provided. Overhung gearing needs robust bearings and a case that will not deform. Moreover, due to the sliding action of a spiral gear mesh, constant lubrication fluid (not grease) is called for.

                                                                                      None of these requirements are easy to meet in a bicycle. Furthermore, a shaft drive with two sets of spiral bevel gears will be a few percent less efficient than a properly designed roller chain drive. Add to that the losses inherent in a multi-speed geared hub and your efficiency probably drops to 90% compared to the 98% a chain drive can achieve.
